Remember

  • You know how to get to the assessment centre and where it is - your appointment letter will usually have a map with it.
  • You need to take your appointment letter and proof of ID (your letter tells you what is acceptable proof of ID).
  • Make sure to ask for a travel reimbursement claim form at the assessment if you do not already have one.
  • Make sure you keep tickets or receipts about bus tickets, parking receipts for both yourself and any companion who accompanies you.
  • To read your appointment letter before you go as it tells you about some other things to take such as new or recent medical letters, glasses, walking aids etc.
  • Write down any questions you may have for the assessor about your appointment.
  • Write down points you want to make, in case they are not covered/asked by the assessor.
  • If you need an interpreter or signer you need to arrange this before the appointment.
  • The assessor does not decide your entitlement to benefit, they send a report to DWP who decides.
  • If you are not sure about what you are being asked, ask the assessor for an explanation and take your time to answer questions.
  • If you are asked to do something physical such as bend/stretch and it causes you any difficulty or pain or you can’t do it, then tell the assessor.
  • If you do not go your benefit will be affected and may stop until you attend a medical assessment. So if you can’t make it to the appointment you must let them know. You will not usually be able to cancel more than one appointment without it affecting your benefit.
